Our Impact: Thriving with FASD

At Harrison's Hope, we believe the journey is so much better when we are not alone. Since our inception on May 6, 2024, we have moved rapidly from vision to action, building a lifeline of support for Canadian families navigating the complexities of Fetal Alcohol Spectrum Disorder (FASD).

2024–2025: A Year of Remarkable Growth

In just over a year, we have expanded our reach to ensure that every caregiver has the resources, advocacy, and community they need to help their loved ones thrive.

Direct Family & Caregiver Support

  • 100+ Caregivers Supported: Providing a foundation of hope and expertise since our inception
  • 48 Families Served: Delivering direct support and specialized services
  • 100% Success in School Advocacy: Successfully supported 3 families in securing critical IPRC identifications and IEPs

Virtual Support Groups: Breaking the Isolation

  • 2024: 65 Virtual Caregiver Support Groups provided for 76 caregivers
  • 2025: 96 Virtual Caregiver Support Groups reached 298 caregivers

Education & Expert Advocacy

Family Stories

Real stories from families whose lives have been transformed by Harrison's Hope.

From Crisis to Connection: Leo's Story

When Leo went missing, his parents Sarah and Mark felt profound helplessness. Like many youth with FASD, Leo's challenges with impulsivity made him an easy target for predators. He was scouted by a local sex-trafficking ring.

How Harrison's Hope Stepped In: We acted as the bridge between the family and a local anti-sex trafficking organization, explaining Leo's neurodiversity and ensuring de-escalation techniques appropriate for FASD.

The Impact: Within 72 hours, Leo was located and safely returned. Today, he has a "Safety Roadmap" and a community that understands his brain. "Without Harrison's Hope, we wouldn't have known where to turn. They gave us the tools to keep him safe." — Sarah, Leo's Mom

Breaking the Cycle: Maya's Journey to an IEP

For two years, Maya's parents felt like they were shouting into a void. Despite her FASD diagnosis, the school board repeatedly denied Maya an IPRC meeting, claiming her academic scores were "too high" for specialized support.

The Turning Point: Harrison's Hope gathered Maya's medical history, translated it into classroom-ready language, and joined the negotiation table. We secured specific accommodations including sensory breaks and visual schedules.

A New Chapter: Within three months, Maya was formally identified by the IPRC with a robust IEP. "Harrison's Hope changed the trajectory of Maya's life. They gave us the expertise to make the school see our daughter for who she truly is." — David, Maya's Father

Justice Through Understanding: Marcus's Path Forward

By age 20, Marcus had been through the court system three times. To the legal system, he was a "repeat offender." To his family, Marcus was a young man with FASD whose memory deficits made it impossible to comply with standard probation terms.

The Harrison's Hope Intervention: We prepared a comprehensive FASD Impact Statement explaining how prenatal alcohol exposure affected his executive functioning. We advocated for a diversion program with a Success Coach.

A Fairer Outcome: Instead of jail, Marcus received a conditional discharge with FASD-specific supports. Today, he works part-time and hasn't had a legal run-in in over a year. "Harrison's Hope quite literally saved his future." — Marcus's Family

About Harrison's Hope

Harrison's Hope is a non-profit organization founded on the principle of lifelong support for individuals and families living with FASD. Harrison's Hope recognizes the need for a continuum of services that starts with prevention, education and extends to senior care, fostering inclusion and interdependence.

For more information: harrisonshope.ca
